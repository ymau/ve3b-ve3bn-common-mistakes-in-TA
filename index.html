G) Anticipatory-it Structure

Master the "It is..." structure for clearer writing

üìö Common Examples

‚ùå Common Mistakes ‚úÖ Revised Version
Our colleagues could be difficult to make arrangements. It might be difficult for our colleagues to make arrangements.
The new computers are cost-effective. It is cost-effective to buy new computers.
OR
Buying new computers is cost-effective.
Our customers are important to attract. It is important to attract our customers.

üí° Key Tips

‚úì Use "It is + adjective + to..." for general statements

‚úì Pattern: It is [adjective] (for someone) to [do something]

‚úì Examples: "It is difficult to...", "It is important to...", "It is cost-effective to..."

‚úì Alternative: Use gerund (-ing) as subject: "Buying computers is cost-effective"

H) Misuse of Connectives

üìö Common Examples

‚ùå Common Mistakes ‚úÖ Revised Version

üí° Key Tips

‚úì Use "Also" or "Furthermore" to add supporting ideas
